8 Streams - All Exports

Innovation Economics

This stream focuses on future export opportunities based on macro economic trends. With an emphasis on the link between macroeconomics and export performance, each session will provide an overview of global trends including the ripple effect of policy decisions on export performance.

Launch Export

Providing practical advice for entering a new market, this stream will highlight a systematic approach to getting export ready. It will provide insights on how to overcome pitfalls and barriers to trade. It is designed for those who are looking to expand or export into international markets for the first time.

Supply Chain

With increasing pressure to maintain competitiveness, streamlining the supply chain has never been so critical in todayâ€™s global economy. This stream will focus on the efficiency opportunities within the supply chain, including how to optimise your existing strategy, mitigate risk and manage partners.

Export +

This stream focuses on the companies who are looking to increase their global footprint. Diversification is critical for exporters who want to mitigate risk and exposure to economic conditions. This stream will provide best practice examples of how to expand into new and emerging markets.

Strategy, Finance & Risk

Unstable currencies, sovereign state crises and changes in the global environment have a direct impact on risk profiles and strategy decisions. This stream is critical for those with significant bottom line control, focussing on all aspects of finance strategy and planning while providing insights on overcoming challenges.

LifeSciences

This stream will look at current challenges and future innovations, from consumerisation to compliance to improving distribution and speed to market. With Ireland as the home of 9 of the top 10 pharma/ biotech companies, this is an unmissable stream for senior execs within the life sciences division.

Service Economy

The world economy is increasingly characterised as a service economy. Disseminating services worldwide is becoming faster and easier, but also more competitive. This stream will cover everything from cutting through a saturated market, to overcoming legal implications, licensing, localisation and data protection challenges.

Food & Drink

The food and drink sector has seen a number of significant changes over the last few years, from shifting commodity prices, easing food restrictions, to increasingly complex customs controls. This stream will demonstrate both best practice and process improvement to maximise sector performance.
